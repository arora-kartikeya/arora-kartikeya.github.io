Quantum Algorithms Research

Developing novel quantum algorithms for optimization, search, and simulation problems with focus on achieving quantum advantage over classical approaches.

Research > Quantum Algorithms

Research Overview

My quantum algorithms research focuses on developing and analyzing quantum algorithms that can provide computational advantages over their classical counterparts. This work spans theoretical analysis, algorithm design, and practical implementation considerations for near-term quantum devices.

Key Research Areas

Graph Algorithms

Developing quantum algorithms for graph connectivity, shortest paths, and network analysis problems with exponential speedup potential.

Recent Work:

  • Quantum walk-based algorithms for graph connectivity
  • Exponential speedup for certain graph isomorphism problems
  • Hybrid classical-quantum approaches for large graphs

Optimization

Creating quantum optimization algorithms for combinatorial and continuous optimization problems using variational approaches.

Recent Work:

  • Novel QAOA variants for MaxCut problems
  • Quantum approximate optimization with noise resilience
  • Portfolio optimization using quantum annealing

Search Algorithms

Extending Grover's algorithm and amplitude amplification techniques for structured search problems.

Recent Work:

  • Amplitude amplification for quantum machine learning
  • Search in unstructured databases with quantum parallelization
  • Fixed-point quantum search algorithms

Current Projects

Quantum Graph Connectivity

Investigating quantum algorithms for determining graph connectivity with potential exponential speedup over classical methods. This work explores quantum walks on graphs and their applications to connectivity problems.

Ongoing

Collaborators: USC Quantum Computing Lab, IBM Research

Funding: NSF Graduate Research Fellowship

Timeline: 2023-2025

Hybrid Quantum-Classical Optimization

Developing algorithms that combine classical heuristics with quantum optimization for large-scale problems. Focus on near-term quantum devices with limited coherence times.

Ongoing

Collaborators: Google Quantum AI, USC

Applications: Supply chain optimization, financial portfolio management

Timeline: 2024-2026
