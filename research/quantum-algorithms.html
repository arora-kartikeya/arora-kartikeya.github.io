Quantum Algorithms Research

Developing novel quantum algorithms that provide computational advantages over classical approaches, with focus on optimization, graph problems, and search algorithms.

Research Overview

Quantum algorithms represent one of the most promising applications of quantum computing, offering the potential for exponential speedups over classical algorithms for specific problem classes. My research focuses on developing practical quantum algorithms that can demonstrate quantum advantage on both near-term and fault-tolerant quantum devices.

Quantum Graph Algorithms

Graph problems are fundamental in computer science and have applications across numerous domains. I am developing quantum algorithms for several important graph problems:

  • Connectivity Problems: Quantum algorithms for determining graph connectivity with improved time complexity
  • Shortest Path Algorithms: Novel approaches to finding shortest paths in weighted graphs
  • Maximum Flow: Quantum algorithms for network flow optimization problems

Key Publications

  • "Efficient Quantum Algorithms for Graph Problems" - arXiv preprint (2024)
  • "Quantum Speedup for Graph Connectivity" - In preparation

Optimization Algorithms

Optimization is a core area where quantum algorithms show promise. I work on both theoretical aspects and practical implementations of quantum optimization algorithms:

  • Combinatorial Optimization: QAOA and its variants for NP-hard problems
  • Continuous Optimization: Quantum approaches to nonlinear programming
  • Hybrid Algorithms: Classical-quantum hybrid optimization strategies

Search and Database Algorithms

Building on Grover's algorithm, I explore generalizations and improvements to quantum search algorithms:

  • Amplitude Amplification: Generalizations of Grover's algorithm for various problems
  • Structured Search: Quantum algorithms for searching with additional structure
  • Database Applications: Practical implementations for database query optimization

Current Projects

Quantum Graph Connectivity (Ongoing)

Developing quantum algorithms for graph connectivity problems that achieve exponential speedup over classical algorithms. This work involves novel techniques in quantum walk algorithms and quantum data structures.

Hybrid Optimization Framework (Planning)

Creating a unified framework for hybrid classical-quantum optimization that can automatically determine the optimal division of work between classical and quantum components.
