Biography

I am a PhD student in Electrical Engineering at the University of Southern California, where I conduct research in quantum computing theory under the guidance of leading experts in the field. My academic journey has been driven by a deep fascination with the intersection of quantum mechanics and computation.

My research focuses on developing novel quantum algorithms that can provide computational advantages over classical approaches. I am particularly interested in optimization problems, graph algorithms, and quantum complexity theory. Through my work, I aim to bridge the gap between theoretical quantum computing concepts and practical implementations on near-term quantum devices.

Beyond research, I am passionate about education and outreach. I believe in making quantum computing accessible to a broader audience through comprehensive tutorials and clear explanations of complex concepts. My goal is to contribute to the growing quantum computing community by sharing knowledge and fostering collaboration.
