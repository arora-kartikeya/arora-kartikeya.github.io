About Me

My journey in quantum computing and research

Biography

I am a PhD student in Electrical Engineering at the University of Southern California, where I focus on the theoretical foundations of quantum computing. My research interests span quantum algorithms, quantum error correction, and quantum information theory.

Before joining USC, I completed my undergraduate studies in Electrical Engineering, where I first discovered my passion for quantum mechanics and its applications to computation. This fascination led me to pursue graduate studies in quantum computing, a field that sits at the intersection of physics, computer science, and electrical engineering.

My current research focuses on developing efficient quantum algorithms for optimization problems and investigating novel approaches to quantum error correction that could enable practical quantum computing systems.
