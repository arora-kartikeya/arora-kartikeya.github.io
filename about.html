About Me

Welcome to my academic portfolio! I am a PhD student in Physics at the University of Southern California, specializing in quantum computing research. My work focuses on developing novel quantum algorithms and exploring the fundamental limits of quantum information processing.

I am passionate about making quantum computing accessible through education and outreach. Through this website, I share tutorials, research insights, and resources for students and researchers interested in quantum computing and quantum physics.

My research is supervised by Prof. Daniel Lidar in the USC Center for Quantum Information Science & Technology, where I collaborate with an interdisciplinary team of physicists, computer scientists, and engineers to push the boundaries of quantum computation.

Background

Prior to joining USC, I completed my undergraduate studies in Physics at the Indian Institute of Technology Delhi, where I first became fascinated with quantum mechanics and its computational applications. My journey into quantum computing began with a summer research project on quantum error correction, which sparked my interest in pursuing graduate studies in this field.

Current Focus

My current research focuses on several key areas in quantum computing, with particular emphasis on practical implementations for near-term quantum devices. I work extensively with superconducting quantum processors and trapped ion systems, developing algorithms that can operate effectively despite current hardware limitations.

Teaching & Mentoring

I am actively involved in teaching and mentoring undergraduate students interested in quantum physics. I regularly lead discussion sections for quantum mechanics courses and supervise undergraduate research projects. I also organize the weekly Quantum Computing Student Seminar at USC.
