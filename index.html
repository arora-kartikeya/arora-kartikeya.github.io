About Me

Welcome! I am a PhD student in Physics at the University of Southern California, specializing in quantum computing research. My work focuses on developing novel quantum algorithms and exploring the fundamental limits of quantum information processing.

I am passionate about making quantum computing accessible through education and outreach. Through this website, I share tutorials, research insights, and resources for students and researchers interested in quantum technologies.

Featured Research

Quantum Error Correction for NISQ Devices

Developing practical quantum error correction schemes for near-term quantum computers, focusing on surface codes and novel decoding algorithms.

Variational Quantum Algorithms

Investigating trainability and performance of VQE for quantum chemistry, with emphasis on barren plateau mitigation strategies.

Quantum Machine Learning

Exploring quantum advantage in ML tasks through quantum neural networks and kernel methods on real quantum hardware.
