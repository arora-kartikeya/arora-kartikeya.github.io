Research Overview

My research focuses on advancing quantum computing through both theoretical developments and practical implementations. I work on several interconnected projects that aim to make quantum computation more reliable, efficient, and accessible for real-world applications.

Current Research Projects

Quantum Error Correction for NISQ Devices

Developing practical quantum error correction schemes for near-term quantum computers, focusing on surface codes and their variants for superconducting qubit platforms.

Key Contributions:

  • Novel decoding algorithms with 40% reduction in computational overhead
  • Threshold improvement from 0.8% to 1.2% for superconducting processors

Variational Quantum Algorithms

Investigating the trainability and performance of variational quantum eigensolvers for quantum chemistry applications, with emphasis on barren plateau mitigation strategies.

Research Focus:

  • Parameter initialization strategies for VQE circuits
  • Gradient-free optimization methods
  • Molecular simulation with 20+ qubit systems
  • Noise-resilient variational algorithms

Impact:

Successfully demonstrated 10x improvement in convergence rates for molecular ground state problems
